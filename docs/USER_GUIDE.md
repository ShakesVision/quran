## Getting Started

### What is Quran Hifz Helper?

Quran Hifz Helper is a free, open-source Progressive Web App (PWA) for reading, memorizing, and exploring the Holy Quran. It works offline, looks like a native app, and is available on every platform — Android, iOS, Windows, Mac, and Linux.

### How to Install

#### 📱 Android — Play Store
1. Open the Google Play Store on your device.
2. Search for **"Quran Hifz Helper"** by Shakeeb Ahmad.
3. Tap **Install**.
4. Open the app and start reading!

#### 📱 Android — PWA (Alternative)
1. Open **Chrome** and go to **https://quran.shakeeb.in**.
2. Tap the **three-dot menu** (⋮) in the top right.
3. Tap **"Add to Home Screen"** or **"Install App"**.
4. The app icon will appear on your home screen.

#### 🍎 iPhone / iPad — PWA
1. Open **Safari** and go to **https://quran.shakeeb.in**.
2. Tap the **Share button** (⬆) at the bottom.
3. Scroll down and tap **"Add to Home Screen"**.
4. Tap **Add**. The app icon will appear on your home screen.

#### 💻 Windows / Mac / Linux — PWA
1. Open **Chrome** or **Edge** and go to **https://quran.shakeeb.in**.
2. Click the **install icon** (⊕) in the address bar, or go to Menu → "Install Quran Hifz Helper".
3. Click **Install**. The app will open in its own window.

## Home Screen

When you open the app, you'll see the **Home Dashboard** with everything at a glance.

### Banner
A promotional or informational banner may appear at the top. Swipe left/right if there are multiple banners. Tap the button (if shown) to take action.

### Read & Learn Section

| Button | What it Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Full Quran** | Open the complete Quran reader. Tap the ▼ icon to choose a different text source. |
| **Browse** | See all 30 Juz and 114 Surahs in a grid. Tap any to start reading. |
| **Scanned** | View high-quality scanned images of physical mushafs. |
| **Listen** | Listen to audio recitation of any surah by famous Qaris. |
| **Memorize** | Track your hifz progress with spaced repetition review system. |

### Discover
Tap the **Discover** card to enter a beautiful, Instagram-like experience. Swipe up to see random ayahs with translations on stunning card designs.

### Daily Targets
Set your daily reading and memorization goals:
1. Tap the **⚙** icon next to "Daily Target".
2. Set your **daily page target** (tilawat), **daily ayah target** (hifz), **number of khatm**, and **target days**.
3. Tap **Save**.
4. Tap the tilawat or hifz card to **log your progress** each day.
5. Watch your weekly heatmap and streak grow!

### Dark Mode
Tap the **🌗 contrast icon** in the top right to toggle dark mode.

## Reading the Quran

### Opening the Reader
- Tap **Full Quran** to read the entire Quran page by page.
- Tap **Browse** → select a Juz or Surah to read a specific section.
- Share any URL like `quran.shakeeb.in/surah/2/ayah/255` to jump directly to Ayat ul Kursi.

### Navigating Pages
- **Swipe left/right** to turn pages (Arabic RTL: swipe right = next page).
- Use the **◀ ▶ arrows** in the bottom toolbar.
- Type a page number in the **page input field**.
- Use the **⋮** menu in the bottom right for "First page" / "Last page".

### Font Size
- Tap **− / +** buttons in the bottom toolbar or in the settings sidebar.
- The current size is shown as a number badge.

### Immersive Mode
- Tap the **↕ expand icon** in the top toolbar to hide all toolbars.
- Tap anywhere on the text to toggle toolbars back.

### Settings (⚙ Sidebar)
Tap the **settings** icon in the top toolbar to open the sidebar:

- **Quick Toggles**: Immersive, Translate, Inline, Tajweed.
- **Tajweed**: Toggle tajweed color highlighting. See the color legend below. Tap the 🎨 icon to customize colors for each rule.
- **Font Size**: Adjust text size.
- **Tatweel**: Toggle Arabic text stretching (kashida justification).
- **Word-by-Word**: Show English meaning under each Arabic word.
- **Inline Translation**: Show English or Urdu translation below each line.
- **Themes**: Choose from 10 beautiful reading themes.
- **Custom Background**: Enter any CSS color, gradient, or image URL.
- **Custom Text Color**: Change text color to any value.
- **Qari**: Choose a reciter for audio playback.

### Scan View
Toggle between Unicode text and scanned page images. Pinch-to-zoom on scanned images.

### Long-Press Options
Long-press (hold) on any line to see options:
- **Play Audio**: Listen to this ayah.
- **Ayah Details**: Open translation/tafseer modal.
- **Bookmark**: Save this line with a note.
- **Select Text**: Enable native text selection to copy multiple lines.

### Bookmarks
- Tap the **🔖 bookmark icon** in the top toolbar to view all bookmarks.
- Your current page is automatically bookmarked.
- Organize bookmarks in folders.

### Search
- Tap the **🔍 search icon** to search for any Arabic text across all pages.

## Tafseer / Translation Modal

When you tap an ayah in the reader (or use the long-press menu):

1. A bottom-sheet modal opens with the **Arabic text** at the top.
2. **Word by Word**: Tap to expand — see each word's meaning and transliteration. Tap any word to hear its pronunciation.
3. **Translations**: Multiple English and Urdu translations shown in colored blocks. Tap to expand/collapse each.
4. **Reorder Translations**: Tap the ⚙ icon in the footer to enter config mode. Use ↑/↓ arrows to reorder, 👁 to show/hide.
5. **Tafsir**: Tap buttons to load Ibn Kathir (Arabic/Urdu) or Ma'arif ul Quran (English).
6. **Navigate**: Use ◀ ▶ to go to previous/next ayah, or swipe left/right.
7. **External Link**: Tap 🌐 to open on tafsir.app for full commentary.
8. **Close**: Tap ✕ or press the back button.

## Discover (Ayah Reels)

1. From the home screen, tap **Discover**.
2. Beautiful full-screen cards appear with a random ayah.
3. **Swipe up** to see the next ayah, **swipe down** for the previous.
4. Each card shows the Arabic text, Urdu translation, and English translation.
5. **Share** (↗): Copy the ayah with translations to clipboard.
6. **Read** (📖): Jump to this ayah in the Quran reader.
7. **Settings** (🌐): Choose your preferred English and Urdu translators.
8. Press **✕** to go back to the home screen.

---

## Memorize (Hifz Tracker)

1. From the home screen, tap **Memorize**.
2. Choose tracking mode: **Juz** (30 items) or **Surah** (114 items).
3. Tap any juz/surah to update your memorization progress.
4. Complete items trigger a **confetti celebration**! 🎉
5. **Due for Review** shows items that need revision based on spaced repetition intervals.
6. **Weekly Heatmap** shows your review activity this week.
7. **Mastery levels**: Items progress from New → Learning → Reviewing → Mastered.
8. Stay consistent — the app tracks your **streak** and shows motivational quotes.

### Recommended Chapters
Quick-start your memorization with commonly memorized surahs: Al-Fatiha, Ya-Sin, Ar-Rahman, Al-Waqiah, Al-Mulk, Al-Kahf.

## Listen (Audio Player)

1. From the home screen, tap **Listen**.
2. Browse or **search** for a surah.
3. Tap a surah name to start playing.
4. Choose your preferred **Qari** from the dropdown at the top.
5. Audio controls (play/pause/seek) appear below the surah list.
6. Audio playback continues even when browsing other parts of the app.

## Scanned Mushaf

1. From the home screen, tap **Scanned**.
2. Choose a **scanned source** from the dropdown:
   - 15-Line Saudi Mushaf
   - 16-Line Darussalam
   - 13-Line Taj Company
   - 16, 17, 18, or 21-Line Taj Company
3. Navigate pages with ◀ ▶ buttons or page number input.
4. **Pinch-to-zoom** for closer inspection.
5. For the 15-line Saudi source, use **Juz** and **Surah** jump controls at the top.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does the app work offline?**  
A: Yes! After the first load, all core reading features work offline. Audio and some translations may need internet.

**Q: How do I change the text source (15-line vs 16-line)?**  
A: On the home screen, use the mushaf source selector. Or in the reader, open settings and change the source.

**Q: Why does some text look different?**  
A: Different text sources use different fonts. Archive sources use Muhammadi font, Quran.com sources use IndoPak Waqf Lazim font.

**Q: How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?**  
A: Tap "Feedback" in the footer of the home page to reach us on Telegram.

**Q: Is the app free?**  
A: Yes, completely free and open-source.
